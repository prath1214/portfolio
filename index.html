  • Title - Comparative Study of EV Battery Cooling System Using Liquid Flow-Channel
  • Project Advisor - Dr. Mahesh Joshi
    • An EV battery cooling system with liquid cooling keeps the battery in an electric vehicle from getting too hot. It works like this: there are tubes filled with liquid that run near or around the battery cells. When the battery starts to get too warm, the liquid flows through these tubes, absorbing the heat from the battery and carrying it away. This helps the battery stay at a safe temperature, which is important for its performance and longevity. Think of it like a cool drink on a hot day it helps keep things from overheating.

  • Title - Comparative Study of Battery Technologies for Electric Vehicles.
  • Project Advisor - Dr. Mahesh Joshi
  • Problem Statement - EV battery technologies‚Äîsuch as Li-ion, Solid-state based on energy density, lifecycle, cost, and safety. It aims to identify the most efficient and sustainable solution for next-generation electric mobility.
  • Introduction - Electric vehicles (EVs) have emerged as a sustainable alternative to conventional internal combustion engine vehicles. As the world moves toward greener transportation, the importance of efficient and reliable battery systems becomes critical. Batteries are the heart of EVs, directly impacting range, performance, cost, and safety. This case study presents a comparative analysis of prominent battery technologies used in EVs, especially from a mechanical engineering perspective.
  • Objectives
    • To compare different battery technologies used in Evs. To evaluate them based on performance, cost, safety, and other key parameters. To identify mechanical engineering challenges and opportunities in EV battery systems.
  • Conclusion - Battery technology directly affects the efficiency, affordability, and safety of electric vehicles. Interdisciplinary collaboration-especially among mechanical, electrical, and materials engineers-is essential for innovation in this field. Mechanical engineers, in particular, have opportunities to improve battery packaging, cooling systems, and overall design for better EV performance.
